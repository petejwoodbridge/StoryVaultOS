# World Bible — Overview

## Logline
A marine acoustics researcher who survived listening to a mysterious deep-ocean signal must
decode what it said — before the institute that classified it erases the last evidence it
ever existed.

## Premise
Twelve months after the Pacific Exclusion Zone transmission known as "The Signal" was detected
and immediately classified by the Arcadia Institute, the only researcher who heard the full
sequence is placed on administrative leave. Armed with nothing but her own altered memory and
a waterproof notebook of handwritten acoustic notations, Alex Chen begins a quiet investigation
into what the Signal said, who else has heard it, and why the three other people who were in
the room that day have each, in different ways, vanished.

## Theme
The story is about what we do with knowledge that was never meant for us — and whether
understanding something dangerous is the same as being responsible for it.

## Tone
Precise. Clinical on the surface, with mounting dread underneath. The horror is procedural:
every door closes with a bureaucratic click. Think *All the President's Men* if the conspiracy
were geological.

## Genre
Near-future sci-fi thriller. Set approximately 15 years from now — the technology is an
extrapolation of current acoustic neuroscience and deep-sea monitoring, nothing fantastical.

## Emotional Core
Alex is not trying to save the world. She is trying to understand what was said to her —
and by extension, whether she was meant to be the one to hear it. That's the engine.
